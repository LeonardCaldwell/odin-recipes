Ingredients

Roti

  • 450g (3 cups) Roti flour or plain flour
  • 1 tspn salt
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 1 tblspn Ghee or oil
  • 1/2 cup oil

Filling

  • 300g Minced Lamb
  • 2cm piece Ginger chopped
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 1 tspn chilli powder
  • 1/2 tspn tumeric
  • 1/4 tspn pepper, freshly ground
  • 1 clove garlic, chopped/grated
  • 1 stalk celery, finely chopped
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 tspn garam maslal
  • salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2 green chilli, seeded and sliced (optional)
  • 2 tblspn coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1 cup fresh peas
  • 3-4 eggs, beaten

Method

Roti

  1. Sift flour and salt together.
  2. Rub in ghee or oil and add water all at once and combine to a soft dough.
  3. Knead dough for about 10 mins until soft, smooth, and elastic. Depending on the flour, you may deed to add less, the 'flour' will tell you!
  4. Divide dough into 10 equal balls and place them in a bowl with enough oil to cover.
  5. Rest for atleast 1 hour.

Filling

  1. Heat 2 tblspns ghee in a frying pan.
  2. Fry ginger and diced onion until fragrant.
  3. Add all the spices except the garam masala and fry for a few minutes
  4. Add the garlic and celeery.
  5. Now add the sliced onion and cook adding a bit of oil if it gets too dry.
  6. Add meat and salt and stir until the meat changes colour, breaking up the lumps of meat.
  7. Add the garam masala and continue to cook, stirring until all the meat is brown, caramelised.
  8. Add water and cover.
  9. When the meat is cooked, add green chilli slices.
  10. Reduce heat and simmer until mixture appears dry (the moisture has cooked out).
  11. Allow to cool. Add peas and coriander.
  12. Season to taste.

Assembly

  1. Pre heat a griddle pan.
  2. Take the balls out of the oil and grease down the flat surface with the oil, then roll out as thin as filo pastry, not worrying if you get a few holes. You just need the middle section with out holes.
  3. Lightly grease the pan and carefully drape the dough over the rolling pin and place it on the hot griddle.
  4. Working quickly as the dough is very fine and will cook quickly, spoon on beaten egg and spread over the centre with the back of a spoon.
  5. Spoon over some of the meat mix and sliced onion.
  6. Fold over all the edges and enclose the filling like an envelope.
  7. Turn it over to cook the other side, brushing the pan with oil or ghee as you go.
  8. Keep frying and turning until both sides are crispy and golden in colour.
  9. Serve hot with cirry, pickled onions or just as is.
